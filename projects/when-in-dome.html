When in Dome

Role UX/Service Designer
Timeline 3 months (2025)
Team Solo project

The Problem

A regional health service was experiencing high rates of missed appointments and patient frustration with their booking system. The existing process required multiple phone calls, long wait times, and offered limited flexibility for rescheduling.

Research revealed that 30% of patients missed appointments due to confusion about booking details, and staff spent an average of 45 minutes per day managing appointment-related phone calls. The system needed to serve a diverse demographic, including elderly patients with varying levels of digital literacy.

Research findings / User journey map

Approach

I conducted user research with 24 patients and 8 administrative staff members to understand pain points in the current system. Key insights included:

  • Patients wanted visual confirmation of their appointments
  • Reminders needed to be multi-channel (SMS, email, phone)
  • The system needed to feel simple and trustworthy
  • Staff needed efficient ways to manage cancellations and rescheduling

Through co-design workshops and iterative prototyping, I developed a service blueprint that mapped the entire appointment journey, identifying opportunities for digital intervention while maintaining human touchpoints for those who needed them.

Design iterations / Prototypes

The design process involved testing three different approaches with users, ranging from a fully automated system to a hybrid model. The hybrid approach tested best, balancing efficiency with the personal care patients expected from healthcare services.

Final interface / Key screens

Outcome

The redesigned system launched as a pilot program in two clinics, with measurable improvements within the first three months:

  • 42% reduction in missed appointments
  • 65% decrease in appointment-related phone calls
  • 88% patient satisfaction rating (up from 62%)
  • Average booking time reduced from 8 minutes to 2 minutes

The service is now being rolled out across the entire health network. The project demonstrated the importance of designing for accessibility and maintaining human connection in digital healthcare experiences.

Key learnings included the value of service design thinking in digital products, the importance of designing for the full ecosystem (patients, staff, and systems), and the need to validate assumptions through testing with diverse user groups.
